Testimonials

Smriti Mehta speaks more highly of her Guru Trinath Maharana - A highly evolved Guru like him can do a lot to popularize this art further.

Times of India (Bombay)

Guru Trinath Maharana, one of the most talented conductors in Delhi pronounced the boles effectively and played the Pakhawaj with gusto.

The Hindustan Times

Maharana's deft strokes on the Pakhawaj were a delight.

The Statesman

Trinath conducted the recital with competence he also played the Pakhawaj complementarily.

The Statesman

"the most significant contributor was Trinath Maharana on the Pakhawaj. The tonal nuances he coaxed out of the instrument made for percussive fare at its best. Never intrusive, his soft touches embellished the total musical effort. Only from Guru Kelucharan Mohapatra have I been treated to such artistry on the Pakhawaj in an Odissi recital".

The Hindu by Leela Venkataraman

"Trinath Maharana on the pakhawaj proved to be a delight, such was the beauty of his playing."

The Times of India by Ashish Khokar

Without the continuing good work of younger gurus like Trinath Maharana and many others, Odissi might not flourish as vibrant art dance form.

Times of India by Padma Vibhushan Dr. Sonal Mansingh

"Trinath Maharana was excellent on the pakhawaj."

The Hindustan Times by Shanta Serbjeet Singh

"Trinath Maharana on the pakhawaj gave Smruti adequate support."
